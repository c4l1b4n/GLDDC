Walden 3.2.2

By George Dickson

By their own follies they perished, the fools.

Harold drank steadily as his car rocketed up the canyon. Each corner was more sublime than the last, but each of the last equally satisfying. He carried on an old Montanan tradition. Like the Running of the Bulls in Pamplona, it refused to die. Harry's tradition was equally ancient and venerable, but now it was as if the fun was sucked out of it--a procession of giant corks, jammed onto the horns of those enormous beasts of burden clamoring up the cobblestones with fatality and terror screwed into their faces.
